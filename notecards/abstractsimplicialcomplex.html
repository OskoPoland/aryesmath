The standard simplex with which we are particularly familiar with is defined as a collection of vectors in $\mathbb{R}^n$. The simplices have a natual combinatorial structure that is deteremined by the inclusion of lower dimensional simplices into higher dimensional simplices. To generalize the standard real valued abstract simplicial complex we capture this combinatorial behavior. The abstract simplicial complex is defined a vertex set $S$ and a collection of subsets of the vertex set, $B$. $B$ is an abstract simplicial complex if for any $J \in B$ and $I \subseteq J$ then $I \in B$.
